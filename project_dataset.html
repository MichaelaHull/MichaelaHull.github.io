Michaela Hull: Project Dataset

San Francisco Police Department Incidents in 2014

General Information

  • Original Source Data
  • Data on Github
  • Licensing Information: Open Data Commons Public Domain Dedication and License
  • Data Type: Geographical, time series, and categorical (lots of categories)

Why I Want to Work With this Dataset

I have worked a lot in the past with numerical datasets and am interested in trying something new. This data is interesting to me and can also be looked at in a number of different ways--it can be considered in terms of geography (neighborhood or longitude, latitude), it can be looked at as a time series, by the entire year, by day of the week, month of the year, or time of day for potential trends within these time periods. These can also be broken up by category. For example, are certain neighborhoods more likely to have certain types of crimes? It would also be interesting to have this be interactive and to allow the viewer to select the variables.

This is a pretty large dataset with 150,185 observations and 13 variables. The original dataset is for all crime since 2003, but since that was clearly too large (it would not have been allowed on GitHub), I filtered it down to one year. If this is still too large, I can probably filter it down to a single month.
